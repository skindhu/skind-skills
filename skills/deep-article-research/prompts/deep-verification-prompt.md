## Research Mission

For EACH argument identified above, conduct thorough web research to:

1. **Verify factual claims** — Are the data points, statistics, and facts cited in the article accurate?
2. **Check logical validity** — Does the evidence actually support the conclusions drawn?
3. **Find counter-evidence** — What contradicting data or expert opinions exist?
4. **Identify context gaps** — What important context does the article omit?
5. **Assess timeliness** — Are the claims still valid given the latest developments?

## Research Focus Areas

### 1. Factual Accuracy Verification
- Cross-reference specific data points, statistics, and quotes
- Verify cited sources actually say what the article claims
- Check if numbers are current or outdated

### 2. Expert & Institutional Perspectives
- Search for expert opinions that support or contradict the arguments
- Look for institutional reports (think tanks, research organizations, government agencies)
- Find academic research relevant to the claims

### 3. Counter-Narrative Search
- Actively search for opposing viewpoints and counter-arguments
- Identify what the article's critics would say
- Find alternative explanations for the same phenomena

### 4. Latest Developments
- Search for the most recent news and data on the topic
- Check if any developments since the article's publication affect its arguments
- Identify emerging trends that the article may have missed

### 5. Source Credibility Assessment
- Evaluate the reliability of sources cited in the article
- Check the author's track record and expertise
- Assess the publication's editorial standards and potential biases
